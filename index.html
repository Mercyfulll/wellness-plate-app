How it works

The Wellness Plate Mental Health Test

mental health test is designed to assist you in monitoring and managing your mental well-being by assessing your stress and mood levels over time. The test aims to provide insights into your mental health status and enables you to track changes over time. Engaging with our mental health test is a straightforward process: Taking our mental health test is easy:

  • Complete the test by responding to a series of questions or statements related to your stress and mood.
  • Understand your results by interpreting your stress and mood levels within the context of our scoring ranges.

What the results mean

The results provide insights into the state of your mental health and offer guidance on improving your well-being through a recommended diet that has been proven to alleviate stress and promote mental wellness.

If your score is:
  • 1-15:

    Your score indicates a low level of stress and favorable mood. You are in a relatively healthy mental state.

  • 16-30:

    Your score suggests a moderate level of stress and mood fluctuations. It's important to implement strategies to manage stress and support your mental well-being.

  • 31-45:

    Your score signifies a high level of stress and potentially challenging mood patterns. It is crucial to prioritize self-care, seek support, and consider professional assistance if needed.

The DASH diet

The DASH (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) diet can help lower high blood pressure and improve overall health. It has been shown to potentially reduce symptoms of depression, alleviate stress, and boost mood. By emphasizing nutrient-rich foods and limiting unhealthy elements, the DASH diet supports both physical and mental well-being.

Ketogenic Diet

The ketogenic diet is a high-fat, low-carbohydrate eating plan that has been shown to have potential benefits for mental health. Research suggests that ketones produced during ketosis may support brain health, while the diet's stabilizing effect on energy levels can also contribute to mental well-being.
